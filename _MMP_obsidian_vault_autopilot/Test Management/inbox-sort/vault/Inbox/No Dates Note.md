# Focus Timer Techniques

Comparison of popular focus timer methods for deep work sessions.

## Pomodoro Technique

25 minutes focus, 5 minutes break. After 4 cycles, take a 15-30 minute break. Simple and well-known. Works best for tasks you are procrastinating on — the 25-minute commitment feels manageable.

Downside: interrupts flow state. If you are deeply focused at minute 24, the timer pulls you out.

## Flowtime Technique

Work until you naturally lose focus, then take a break proportional to work time (5 min break per 25 min work). No fixed intervals. Better for creative or complex tasks where flow state matters.

Downside: requires self-awareness. Easy to skip breaks and burn out.

## 52-17 Method

52 minutes work, 17 minutes break. Based on a DeskTime study of most productive employees. The longer focus block allows deeper work than Pomodoro.

Downside: the 17-minute break feels long. Hard to fill productively without getting distracted.

## My Setup

Using Flowtime for coding sessions and Pomodoro for admin tasks (emails, reviews, planning). Tracking with the Toggl app to find my natural rhythm.
